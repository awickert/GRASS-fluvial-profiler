DESCRIPTION

r.fluvial.fastscape evolves a landscape forward in time under rock uplift and detachment-limited fluvial incision, solving the stream-power model 
dz/dt = U - K A^m S^n
where z is elevation, U rock-uplift rate, A drainage area, S channel slope, and K, m, n the stream-power coefficient and exponents. The equation is integrated with the implicit, stack-ordered FastScape algorithm of Braun & Willett (2013), which is unconditionally stable: arbitrarily large timesteps do not cause the solution to blow up.

Each timestep: rock uplift is added to the interior, flow is routed on a depression-filled surface (D8), drainage area and the ordered node stack are computed, and the incision term is solved implicitly from downstream to upstream (closed-form for n = 1, Newton–Raphson otherwise). The solver shares its Braun & Willett flow-routing machinery with r.fluvial.channelheads.

NOTES

The four domain edges are fixed as base level by default (flag -b disables this). Cells are assumed square; for non-square regions the north–south resolution is used and a warning is issued. Per-cell K and U rasters may be supplied with k_map and uplift_map.

At steady state the model reproduces the classic channel slope–area relation S = (U/K)1/n A-m/n.

EXAMPLE

r.fluvial.fastscape input=dem output=dem_evolved \
    k=1e-5 m=0.5 n=1 uplift=1e-3 dt=1000 nsteps=200

REFERENCES

Braun, J. and Willett, S.D. (2013). A very efficient O(n), implicit and parallel method to solve the stream power equation governing fluvial incision and landscape evolution. Geomorphology 180–181, 170–179, doi:10.1016/j.geomorph.2012.10.008.
